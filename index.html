Greenhouse Emissions

The main driver of increasing temperatures is Greenhouse Gas emissions. Levels of Carbon Dioxide, Nitrous Oxide and Methane have all been recorded at record highs in recent years

Hover your cursor over the graph to highlight the amount of GHG emissions.

Energy Consumption

Fossil fuels are major emitters of greenhouse gases and dominate the world's energy consumption. However, producing more renewable energies such as Solar or Wind and limiting the use of Oil, Coal and Gas are key to minimising our emissions and limiting rising temperatures.

Click on the legend or use the selection menu to highlight the energy source.

Australia may not be the highest consumer of fossil fuels per capita, however we still have a lot of work to do to lower our reliance on fossil fuels.

Europe and Brazil are leading the way, consuming significantly less fossil fuels than a majority of the world, as they invest in greener Renewables.

Click on the legend to highlight a region or use the slider to compare different years.

Some countries are acting faster in the face of climate change. Paraguay generates 100% of its electricity from renewable sources, whereas Australia is lagging behind at roughly 21%. We must do better to ensure a greener future.
