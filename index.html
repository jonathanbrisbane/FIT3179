Impacts of Global Energy Production on Climate Change

A Visualisation by Jonathan Brisbane

The Globe is Warming

Global Temperatures have been rising at unprecedented rates in the last 100 years. This has had major repercussions on Earth's natural ecosystems and has led to devastating losses to biodiversity, droughts and environmental changes.

Use the slider to highlight which years have had the highest temperatures. The green line highlights the mean temperature change during this period.

Greenhouse Emissions

The main driver of increasing temperatures is Greenhouse Gas emissions. Levels of Carbon Dioxide, Nitrous Oxide and Methane have all been recorded at record highs in recent years

Energy Production

The world still has a high reliance on fossil fuels which are major emitters of greenhouse gases. However, renewable energies such as Solar or Wind are key to minimising our emissions and limit rising temperatures.

Some countries are acting faster in the face of climate change. Paraguay generates 100% of its electricity from renewable sources, whereas Australia is lagging behind at roughly 20%. We must do better to ensure a greener future.
